# Quant Finance Terms & Concepts

This file is a glossary for beginners and contributors in the Thales Open Quant Initiative.  
It explains common terms and concepts you’ll encounter in the repository.

---

## **Factor**
- **Definition:** A measurable characteristic that explains returns of assets, e.g., value, momentum, volatility.  
- **Why it matters:** Helps identify systematic drivers of performance in portfolios.  

## **Backtest**
- **Definition:** Simulating a trading or investment strategy on historical data.  
- **Why it matters:** Tests a strategy’s performance before using real money.  

## **Portfolio**
- **Definition:** A collection of financial assets held by an investor.  
- **Why it matters:** Balances risk and return across multiple assets.  

## **Risk Metrics**
- **Examples:** Volatility, Value at Risk (VaR), Beta.  
- **Why it matters:** Measures potential losses and helps manage exposure.  

## **Market Microstructure**
- **Definition:** The study of how trades, orders, and liquidity affect prices.  
- **Why it matters:** Understanding execution and market impact improves trading strategies.  

## **Volatility Surface**
- **Definition:** A 3D representation of implied volatility across strikes and maturities for options.  
- **Why it matters:** Crucial for pricing derivatives and understanding market expectations.  

## **Regime Detection**
- **Definition:** Identifying different market states (e.g., high volatility, low volatility) using statistical methods.  
- **Why it matters:** Helps adapt strategies to changing market conditions.  

## **Data Pipeline**
- **Definition:** Steps to collect, clean, process, and store financial data.  
- **Why it matters:** Clean and structured data is essential for reliable analysis.  

## **Alpha**
- **Definition:** Excess return of an investment relative to a benchmark.  
- **Why it matters:** Measures the effectiveness of a strategy beyond market movements.  

## **Beta**
- **Definition:** Sensitivity of a security or portfolio to market movements.  
- **Why it matters:** Indicates systematic risk exposure.  

## **Backtest vs Live Trading**
- **Backtest:** Historical simulation  
- **Live Trading:** Real-money execution  
- **Why it matters:** Not all strategies that perform in backtests succeed in live markets.
